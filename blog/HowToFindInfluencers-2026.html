How To Find Influencers (2026 Update)

by Team Runwae
- Jan 15, 2026
The influencer landscape has transformed dramatically since we first wrote about finding influencers in 2021. Short form video dominates, AI tools have changed discovery, and authenticity matters more than ever. Here is our updated guide for 2026.

This is an updated version of our 2021 article on finding influencers, revised to reflect current best practices and platform changes.

Start With Platform Strategy

In 2021, Instagram was still the default platform for influencer marketing. Today, the landscape is more fragmented. TikTok has matured into a primary marketing channel, YouTube Shorts competes for attention, and LinkedIn has emerged as a serious player for B2B influencer partnerships.

Before searching for influencers, decide where your audience actually spends time. A skincare brand might find success on TikTok with creators who specialize in "get ready with me" content. A software company might see better results partnering with LinkedIn thought leaders. Do not assume Instagram is the answer anymore.

Leverage AI Discovery Tools

The days of manually scrolling through hashtags are over. AI powered influencer discovery platforms can now analyze engagement patterns, audience demographics, brand safety signals, and content authenticity at scale. These tools can identify creators whose audiences genuinely match your target customer profile, not just those with impressive follower counts.

Look for platforms that offer:

  • Audience overlap analysis with your existing customers
  • Fake follower and engagement fraud detection
  • Content sentiment analysis
  • Historical performance data across campaigns

Prioritize Nano and Micro Creators

The shift toward smaller creators has accelerated. Nano influencers (1,000 to 10,000 followers) and micro influencers (10,000 to 100,000 followers) consistently outperform larger accounts in engagement rate and conversion. Their audiences trust their recommendations because the relationship feels personal rather than transactional.

Building a network of 20 micro influencers often delivers better results than a single partnership with a macro influencer at the same total cost. The diversification also reduces risk if any single partnership underperforms.

Look for Content Creators, Not Just Influencers

The distinction between "influencer" and "content creator" matters more now. Many brands are shifting toward partnerships focused on content creation rather than pure promotion. A talented creator can produce authentic content featuring your product that you can repurpose across your own channels, paid ads, and website.

When evaluating potential partners, look at the quality of their content production, not just their reach. A creator with 5,000 followers who produces exceptional video content may be more valuable than one with 50,000 followers posting low effort content.

Tap Your Existing Community

This advice from our original article remains true: your best influencer partners are often already fans of your brand. Use social listening tools to identify people who organically mention your products. Check who tags your brand in content. Look at your most engaged followers.

These organic advocates convert to paid partnerships smoothly because their enthusiasm is already genuine. Their audiences have likely heard them mention your brand before, making any sponsored content feel like a natural extension rather than a sudden pivot.

Verify Authenticity Thoroughly

Influencer fraud has become more sophisticated, so your verification process needs to keep pace. Beyond checking for fake followers, look at:

  • Comment quality and conversation depth
  • Follower growth patterns over time
  • Consistency between engagement rate and follower count
  • Geographic distribution of audience
  • Previous brand partnership performance

Ask potential partners for case studies or performance data from previous campaigns. Legitimate creators will have this information and share it willingly.

Consider Long Term Partnerships

One off sponsored posts are becoming less effective as audiences grow skeptical of obvious advertisements. The brands seeing the best results are building ongoing relationships with creators who become genuine ambassadors over time.

When you find creators who align with your brand, think about how you might work together over months or years rather than a single campaign. These deeper partnerships allow creators to integrate your products naturally into their content, building credibility with their audience through consistent authentic use.

What Has Not Changed

Despite all the platform shifts and new tools, the fundamentals remain the same. The best influencer partnerships are built on genuine alignment of values. Creators who actually use and believe in your product will always outperform those who are just collecting a paycheck. No amount of AI discovery or platform optimization can replace that foundation of authenticity.

"The tools for finding influencers have evolved dramatically, but the principle remains: authentic partnerships built on shared values will always outperform transactional arrangements."
