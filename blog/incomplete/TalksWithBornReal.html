te Influencer pricing is very misunderstood by the industry. The top two misunderstandings are that: Pricing is dependent on the influencer The following and engagement of an influencer should dictate their price point Here’s an explanation for these misunderstandings Influencer marketing is a collaboration. Rightfully so, influencers view themselves as independent entities. They have the control over their direction. Brands don’t understand this: effective influencers view brands the same way that brands view an influencers. Would you pay any two influencer the same rate for an opportunity? Similarly, an influencer will charge brands differently depending on the brand. The most effective influencers don’t price based on the size of the brand, but the ultimate benefit to their audience and their brand. You can’t price an influencer without the brand. Many brands understand that follower count shouldn’t dictate the quality of an influencer for the simple reason that influencers can have “bought” or low quality followers. Brands who equate follower count to a price haven’t yet figured out effective influencer marketing. Consumers are moved by need and credibility. Many consumers who follow celebrities and engage with their content don’t necessarily view them as credible. They don’t respect their opinions or judgement. They follow them for entertainment. Therefore, when they make a post witht heir recommendation for a product, many followers will not pay this much attention, much less be moved to purchase. Brands may rely on the expansive following of influencers to spread awareness even if it’s latent. However, those who rely on latent advertisement and seed planting disregard one simple fact. The volume and variety of content that we consume on a daily basis is making this method increasingly inaccurate. Instead of seeing 5 commercials every 15 minutes, we see a commercial every 10 seconds. The effectiveness of the channel is the true value in influencer marketing. Not the size or relevance. Ask Contact a ton of influencers with a variety of offers. Gauge how their price point varies with their following and engagement. As mentioned earlier, any reports about influencer pricing will be fairly inaccurate due to the situational aspect of influencer pricing and the unstandardized nature of the market as it relates to its scale and ubiquity today. Therefore, the most accurate way to price influencers is by asking. This way, you understand pricing as it relates to you brand because pricing is personal for the most effective influencers. Check The Metrics As mentioned earlier, following and engagement are not the end all for influencer pricing because it doesn’t measure the how much the decisionmaking of those followers is influenced. This is due to a variety of more confounding variables. However, these can be good indicators for establishing a baseline for pricing. Metrics to check include: Followers Check engagement weigh likes, comments, shares presence on other platforms It true that you an influencer can have a signifiant following on one platform and limited influencer on other platforms. However, dedicated following on multiple platforms are signs of a person with real influence. Know your ask story post, feed post, content Be intentional about what you’re looking for and why. Are you looking for content or a story post because you want to engage this influencer, but don’t have the budget for a full post? Do you want a story feature because you want to use the features of instagram stories to take a poll and gain feedback? Do you want a story post to indicate strong belief and a conretecollaboration? You may want the content from the influencer ecause showing the influencer’s support to your following is sufficient to your existing base. This may be true in a situation where you want your existing following to purchase a product as opposed to increasing awareness or acqiuiring new customers. Add tracking add tools to tell how much the influencer contributed to your business growth. This will allow you to continuously improve your price point as you continue to engage influencers. Choosing an influencer right for your brand Audience Let’s get the biggest misconception out of the way right away. Many brands try to find their brand in influencer form. They look for common audiences, styles, values, and industries. Thought this can be effective in many cases, is it not required. In many cases, the contrary can be very effective. An influencer that mirrors the brand is sure to relate to the influencers audience. However, this is not always an effective marketing tactic. Marketing a health product to a group of health experts will be sure to realte to them. However, that same following is sure to be equally aware of you competitors, which may have better positioning than you. This can turn into a war of resources where brand personality is of little importance which not all brands can win. A more clever approach is to collaborate with an influencer who deviates from your brand identity. This can brings your values to the consumer in a way that they can understand it. This allows you to experience the untapped potential of your market. This is also a place where the market is less likely to be saturated. Values Be aware that an influencer’s values may align with yours, but be communicated in a different way. This is often very effective tactic for collaboration because the it intrigues the consumer while satisfying them if the commonality between values is communicated effectively.
